Autonomous Driving Projects

  • 2D and 3D LiDAR SLAM and Localization Algorithms [C++, ROS]

    Implemented 2D LiDAR SLAM algorithm with ICP using RPLiDAR and Raspberry Pi 4. Reproduced 3D LiDAR SLAM (NDT & LOAM-based odometry, factor graph optimization, loop closure) & scan-to-map localization. Following SOTA LiDAR SLAM and localization research closely.

    Learn More

  • Autonomous Driving Algorithms on DIY Self-driving Car [Python]

    Designed and made a Raspberry Pi 4-based self-driving car with monucular camera. Implemented lane detection, EKF localization, landmark-based EKF SLAM, motion planning and control algorithms to make it drive indoor autonomously.

    Learn More

  • Racing and Obstacle Avoidance of Virtual Self-Driving Car [Matlab]

    Led a UM ME599 course project team in conducting free time optimization to shorten lap time of self-driving car while preserving vehicle dynamics constraints. Implemented Model Predictive Control to follow the optimized trajectory and avoid random obstacles at running time.

Work

  • Parking Rack Load Prediction for Steering System [Ford]

    Led accurate parking rack load modeling to enable virtual development of Electric Power Steering (EPS) system. Proposed and validated a novel test-based methodoloy. Achieved SOTA in industry. Won Ford VCSE 2020 Innovation Award.

  • BNA Model to Improve Friction Behavior of Steering System [Ford]

    Created analytical models for Ball Nut Assembly (BNA). Identified the root cause of excessive friction spikes in EPS system with the developed analytical models. Conducted optimization for more robust friction performance of EPS system.

  • Software-based Steering System Failure Backup [Past Work at UM]

    Introduced differential braking to reduce driver’s steering effort from 30+ N·m (unmanageable) to <5 N·m (normal) to reduce risks of crash and injury when EPS system fails. Investigated torque vectoring to downsize EPS motor.
